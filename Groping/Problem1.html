Improved labs for better emergency response: strengthening public health security in Mongolia

During health emergencies, timely information can allow governments to make decisions that save lives. But in 2020, Mongolia faced challenges in getting some of the evidence it needed.

“At the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, it was crucial to determine which variants were causing the disease in Mongolia,” says Naranzul Tsedenbal, head of the Virology Laboratory at Mongolia’s National Center for Communicable Diseases (NCCD). However, the country’s laboratories were not equipped to use the most accurate, up-to-date methods, so samples were sent overseas for genomic sequencing.
